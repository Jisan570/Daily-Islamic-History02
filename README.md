# 🌙 Islamic History Journey - Day 22
> **"Muhammad is not but a messenger. [Other] messengers have passed on before him. So if he was to die or be killed, would you turn back on your heels?..." (Surah Ali 'Imran, 3:144)**

Welcome to Day 22 of my daily Islamic History series. Today, we look into the most sorrowful and testing moment in Islamic history: the passing of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) in 11 AH (632 CE), and how the early Muslim community navigated this ultimate grief.

---

## 🤒 The Beginning of the Final Illness

A few months after returning from the Farewell Pilgrimage, in Safar 11 AH, the Prophet (ﷺ) began experiencing severe headaches and a high fever. As his illness worsened, he requested permission from his wives to stay in the room of **Aisha (RA)**, where she could care for him.

Despite his immense weakness, the Prophet (ﷺ) continued to lead the daily prayers in the mosque for as long as his body allowed. When he became too weak to stand, he gave a clear instruction: *"Tell Abu Bakr to lead the people in prayer."* This subtle command heavily indicated the high status and future leadership of Abu Bakr (RA).

---

## 🕊️ The Final Days and Advice

In his final days, the Prophet (ﷺ) repeatedly gave two crucial pieces of advice to the Ummah:
1. **The Prayers:** He constantly whispered, *"The prayer, the prayer, and those whom your right hands possess (be kind to your servants)."*
2. **Rejection of Shirk:** He warned his followers never to turn his grave into a place of worship or an idol, keeping Monotheism ($Tawhid$) absolute.

On Monday, the 12th of Rabi' al-Awwal, 11 AH, the Prophet's fever briefly broke. He lifted the curtain of Aisha's room and smiled as he watched his companions standing in rows for the dawn prayer. It was his final farewell look at the community he had built.

---

## 💔 The Passing and the Shock in Madinah

Later that afternoon, while resting his head on Aisha's chest, the Prophet (ﷺ) dipped his hand in water, wiped his face, and uttered his final words:
> *"O Allah, [I choose] the highest companionship (Ar-Rafiq al-A'la)."*

With those words, the soul of the greatest man to ever walk the earth departed this world. He was 63 years old.

### ⚖️ The Reaction of the Companions:
The news struck Madinah like a lightning bolt. The city fell into absolute darkness and overwhelming grief. 
* **Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA),** completely blinded by shock and denial, drew his sword and shouted that the Prophet (ﷺ) had not died, but had merely gone to meet Allah like Prophet Musa (AS), and that he would cut off the head of anyone who said otherwise.
* **Abu Bakr (RA),** deeply broken but maintaining incredible emotional resilience, walked into the room, kissed the Prophet's forehead, and then stepped outside to calm the crowd. He recited the timeless words:
  > *"O people! If anyone worshipped Muhammad, let him know that Muhammad is dead. But if anyone worships Allah, let him know that Allah is Alive and never dies."*

Hearing this verse, Umar (RA) fell to his knees in tears, finally accepting the heartbreaking reality.

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways & Lessons

1. **The Mortality of Prophets:** The passing of the Prophet (ﷺ) proved that every human being, no matter how beloved to Allah, must return to their Creator. The message and the Creator survive, even when the Messenger passes.
2. **Crisis Leadership:** Abu Bakr’s calm and firm stance during the greatest crisis the Ummah had ever faced saved the Muslim community from fracturing, ensuring that the legacy of Islam remained stable.
